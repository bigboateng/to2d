# Introduction to to2d

This research site explores control systems, optimization, and computational methods. The focus is on mathematical rigor and practical implementation.

## Mathematical Foundations

Consider a simple optimization problem in two dimensions. Given an objective function $f: \mathbb{R}^2 \to \mathbb{R}$, we seek to find:

$$
\mathbf{x}^* = \arg\min_{\mathbf{x} \in \mathbb{R}^2} f(\mathbf{x})
$$

where $\mathbf{x} = (x_1, x_2)^T$ represents our decision variables.

## Gradient Descent

The gradient descent algorithm provides a first-order method for finding local minima. The update rule is:

$$
\mathbf{x}_{k+1} = \mathbf{x}_k - \alpha_k \nabla f(\mathbf{x}_k)
$$

where $\alpha_k > 0$ is the step size at iteration $k$, and $\nabla f$ denotes the gradient of $f$.

## Control Systems

In linear control theory, we often work with state-space representations:

$$
\begin{aligned}
\dot{\mathbf{x}}(t) &= A\mathbf{x}(t) + B\mathbf{u}(t) \\
\mathbf{y}(t) &= C\mathbf{x}(t) + D\mathbf{u}(t)
\end{aligned}
$$

where:
- $\mathbf{x}(t) \in \mathbb{R}^n$ is the state vector
- $\mathbf{u}(t) \in \mathbb{R}^m$ is the control input
- $\mathbf{y}(t) \in \mathbb{R}^p$ is the output
- $A, B, C, D$ are system matrices of appropriate dimensions

## Lyapunov Stability

A fundamental concept in control theory is Lyapunov stability. For an autonomous system $\dot{\mathbf{x}} = f(\mathbf{x})$ with equilibrium point $\mathbf{x}_e$, we say the equilibrium is stable if there exists a continuously differentiable function $V: \mathbb{R}^n \to \mathbb{R}$ such that:

1. $V(\mathbf{x}_e) = 0$ and $V(\mathbf{x}) > 0$ for all $\mathbf{x} \neq \mathbf{x}_e$
2. $\dot{V}(\mathbf{x}) = \nabla V(\mathbf{x})^T f(\mathbf{x}) \leq 0$

## Convex Optimization

A function $f: \mathbb{R}^n \to \mathbb{R}$ is convex if for all $\mathbf{x}, \mathbf{y} \in \mathbb{R}^n$ and $\lambda \in [0,1]$:

$$
f(\lambda \mathbf{x} + (1-\lambda)\mathbf{y}) \leq \lambda f(\mathbf{x}) + (1-\lambda)f(\mathbf{y})
$$

Convex optimization problems have the remarkable property that any local minimum is also a global minimum.

## Matrix Exponential

The solution to the linear differential equation $\dot{\mathbf{x}} = A\mathbf{x}$ with initial condition $\mathbf{x}(0) = \mathbf{x}_0$ is:

$$
\mathbf{x}(t) = e^{At}\mathbf{x}_0
$$

where the matrix exponential is defined by:

$$
e^{At} = \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} \frac{(At)^k}{k!} = I + At + \frac{(At)^2}{2!} + \frac{(At)^3}{3!} + \cdots
$$
